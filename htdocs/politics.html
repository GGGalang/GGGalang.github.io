POLITICS


POLITICS, a constant topic that quite literally hold the fate of our world, this area is for news and anything related regarding politics, whether it be major global events to the constant local talk about our figures and leaders, especially considering the upcoming elections, as every day more and more people talk about the different leaders and their differing opinions, beliefs and platforms as everyone votes for who they think is the one fit to rule. This news section will mostly talk about Philippinr Politics, although world politics may also find a spot in this section.

Credits: Al Jazeera

Duterte Withdraws from 2020 Senate race

December 14, 2021

After proclaiming retirement from politics just a few weeks ago, incumbent President Duterte declared his position to run for the Senate in the race next year. He has now withdrawn from the race, however, with no clear reason as to why. Duterte's current term will end on June 2022, while his daughter Sara Duterte is hoping to run and win the race for Vice President, with running mate Bongbong Marcos, son of former President Ferdinand Marcos (Al Jazeera, 2021).

Credits: CLIFF VENZON, Nikkei Asia

Marcos Leads in Latest Opinion Poll by Pulse Asia

December 22, 2021

In a recent opinion poll conducted by independent polling outlet Pulse Asia, Bongbong Marcos, whose running mate is the incumbent President's daughter Sara Duterte, is currently leading the opinion polls by a staggering 53%. This is a huge jump from his last 15% (Nikkei Asia, 2021).

Credits: ABS-CBN

Leni Robredo Visits Typhoon Odette Survivors

December 22, 2021

In further attempt to reach and give charity to the victims of the destructive Typhoon Odette, the current Vice President running for President Leni Robredo visits Bais City in Negros Oriental to check up on the victims of the typhoon, as well as give donations (ABS-CBN, 2021).

Bong Go Retires from Presidential Race

November 30, 2021

Duterte's right-hand man and supposedly chosen successor Bong Go has officially withdrawn from the Presidential Race in the upcoming elections. Bong Go stated that it is not yet "his time" after many speculations done by experts giving him a low chance of winning, and after his family not encouraging him to run for the position. Currently, there is no word as to who the incumbent will support now that Bong Go has officialy withdrawn (Al Jazeera, 2021).

Credits: Voltaire F. Domingo/Senate PRIB

Calamity Politics the Lowest Form of Campaigning, According to Lacson

December 17, 2021

Ping Lacson, another candidate in the Presidential Race, states that calamity politics is the lowest form of campaigning. "In fact, I consider it abominable... Election or no election, we assist and help, period" stated the politician, in response to a public call by fellow candidate Manny Pacquiao for all presidential candidates to "put aside politics" and pool resources for the victims of the recent typhoon. He states that if done privately, he would've gladly donated. Since it was done through media, it goes against his principle of calamity politics being the lowest form of campaigning (Inquirer.net, 2021).
