Typhoon Odette Death Toll Swells to 258 according to NDRRMC

December 23, 2021

According to a recent report from the organization of National Disaster and Risk Reduction Management, the official death toll from Typhoon Odette, internationally known as Typhoon Rai, has swelled to 258. Reported with it was at least 568 injured. According to another source the Philippine National Police, over 400 have died from the typhoon. According to the NDRRMC, more than 600,000 have left their homes (Inquirer.net, 2021)

Credits: DOE

Power in 633 'Odette-hit' Municipalities Restored

December 22, 2021

In a recent report by the Department of Energy (DOE), electricity in 633 out of 939 'Odette-hit' municipalities has been restored as of 3pm on Tuesday, December 21. This comprises 78% of all Odette-hit towns. The latest estimate on damages as of the time of writing is around P373 Million. Further reports expect the amount to be higher (Philippine News Agency, 2021).

Credits: Miss Universe Philippines

Miss Universe Philippines Bea Luigi Gomez Ends Run in Top 5 Finish

December 13, 2021

The Philippines' Miss Universe contender Bea Luigi Gomez has ended her Miss Universe journey inside the Top 5 ring of contestants. Former Filipino Beauty Queens congratulated Bea for her finish. India's Harnaaz Sandhu brought home the crown in the event. It was 2018 when Philippines last entered the Top 5 in the Miss Universe competition (CNN Philippines, 2021).

Credits: COA

Quezon City Unseats Makati City as Richest LGU in the Philippines

December 23, 2021

In a recent audit done by the Commission on Audit for financial reports, Mayor Joy Belmonte's Quezon City has unseated Makati City as the richest LGU in the Philippines. Cebu Province ranks as the richest province in our antion however, followed by Batangas and Rizal for 2nd and 3rd respectively (Rappler, 2021).

Credits: Inquirer.net

Biden to Appoint Three Filipinas as Whitehouse Advisers

December 24, 2021

In a recent report from the Whitehouse, President Joseph "Joe" Biden will appoint three Filipino Anericans to the President's Advisory Commission on Asian Americans, Native Hawaiians and Pacific Islanders. The commission inckudes civic leaders from across the country (Rappler, 2021.)
